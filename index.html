The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Bangladesh's digital transformation is revolutionizing industries through initiatives like Digital Bangladesh, focusing on expanding internet connectivity, fostering innovation, and promoting e-governance to drive economic growth and empower citizens.

Green Energy

Bangladesh is embracing green energy to reduce carbon emissions and ensure sustainable development, with significant investments in solar, wind, and renewable energy projects. The country aims to achieve 40% of its energy from renewables by 2041, promoting a cleaner and greener future.

Modern infrastructure

Bangladesh is rapidly developing modern infrastructure, including bridges, highways, and urban transit systems, to boost connectivity and economic growth. Landmark projects like the Padma Bridge and metro rail reflect the nation's commitment to sustainable and innovative development.

"Dr. Muhammad Yunus, a Bangladeshi economist and Nobel Peace Prize laureate, is celebrated as the "Father of Microfinance" for founding the Grameen Bank in 1983. His revolutionary approach of providing small, collateral-free loans to the poor, particularly women, empowered millions in Bangladesh to escape poverty through self-employment and entrepreneurship. His work not only transformed rural development and women's empowerment in Bangladesh but also inspired a global movement for financial inclusion. Dr. Yunus also pioneered the concept of social business, emphasizing enterprises that focus on solving social issues rather than maximizing profits."

Dr. Muhammad Yunus

Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh
